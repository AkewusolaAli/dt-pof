Gender Diversity Analitics

Client Project Overview:
The client faced significant attrition and sought insights into its distribution across departments, commute distance, age groups, educational levels, and tenure.
After cleaning and transforming the data, the dashboard layout was designed based on the conceptual model. The data model was tailored to address all client inquiries. An executive summary visualized the overall attrition. Key findings include that 56% of attrition occurred in the Research and Development department, and 51% were single employees. Dissatisfaction accounted for 31% of attrition, while surprisingly, 19.4% were satisfied employees. Regarding age, 37.5% of attrition were aged 30-39, with the lowest attrition among those under 20. A significant 76.8% of attrition came from employees with 1-10 years of tenure. Despite an employee gender distribution of 49% female and 51% male, attrition was higher among males at 60%.
